Extended Abstract: Distributed Optimization of Multi-Class SVMs

State-of-the-art exact solvers for linear multi-class SVMs require a time complexity of O(d n C^2) and space complexity of O(d C + n C^2). This puts heavy limitations on the amount of classes (denoted C), where these solvers are applicable, especially for high dimensional but sparse data. Motivated by recent interest in multi-class problems with massive numbers of classes, we draft a distributed algorithm where we can employ up to O(C) machines with small computational overhead, while the model is evenly split over all machines. This allows us to compare several classical MC-SVM formulations on an unprecedented scale.
